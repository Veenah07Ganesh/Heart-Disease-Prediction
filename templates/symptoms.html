Symptoms & Causes

Heart disease often develops silently, with symptoms that may not appear until significant damage has already occurred. Many individuals are unaware they have a heart condition until they experience a major event such as a heart attack or stroke. This makes it crucial to recognize early warning signs like chest pain, shortness of breath, fatigue, dizziness, or irregular heartbeat.
Equally important is understanding the root causes behind heart disease. These can include high blood pressure, high cholesterol, diabetes, smoking, poor diet, physical inactivity, obesity, and chronic stress. In some cases, family history and genetics also play a major role in increasing risk. By being informed about these causes and symptoms, individuals can take proactive steps toward prevention, such as regular checkups, ealthy lifestyle choices, and early medical intervention. Recognizing and addressing these factors early can make a life-saving difference.

Symptoms of Heart Disease

Chest pain or
discomfort

A pressure-like, tight, or burning sensation in the chest that may radiate to the arm, neck, jaw, or back, especially during activity or stress.

Shortness of
breath

Uncomfortable breathing or difficulty catching your breath, even at rest or with light physical effort, possibly indicating heart failure or poor circulation.

Fatigue or weakness

Persistent, unexplained tiredness or exhaustion, even with simple tasks, suggesting the heart isn’t supplying enough blood.

Irregular heartbeat

A sensation of fluttering, racing, or skipping beats often noticeable in the chest or throat.

Dizziness or
Lightheadedness

Spinning sensation or near-fainting caused by reduced blood flow to the brain.

Swelling in Legs,
Ankles, or Feet

Fluid accumulation in the lower body, usually due to the heart’s reduced circulation ability.

Causes of Heart Disease

High Blood Pressure
(Hypertension)

Persistent high blood pressure forces the heart to work harder, damaging arteries and increasing the risk of heart attack and stroke.

High Cholesterol
Levels

Too much LDL cholesterol causes plaque buildup inside arteries, reducing blood flow and increasing heart disease risk.

Smoking and Tobacco Use

Tobacco smoke contains chemicals that injure blood vessels and raise the risk of heart disease.

Diabetes

High blood sugar from diabetes damages blood vessels and nerves, especially those controlling the heart.

Obesity and
Inactivity

Excess weight and lack of exercise raise the risk of heart disease by worsening blood pressure, cholesterol, and sugar levels.

Genetics
and Family History

Having close relatives with heart disease raises your own risk, especially if combined with poor lifestyle habits.
