Heart Disease

Heart disease, also known as cardiovascular disease, refers to a broad range of conditions that affect the heart and blood vessels. It is one of the leading causes of death globally, claiming millions of lives each year. Common types of heart disease include coronary artery disease, heart failure, arrhythmia, and congenital heart defects. These conditions can develop silently over time and often remain undetected until serious complications arise, such as heart attacks or strokes.

The primary risk factors for heart disease include high blood pressure, high cholesterol, diabetes, obesity, smoking, poor diet, physical inactivity, and excessive alcohol consumption. Genetics and age also play significant roles in determining an individual's risk level. Fortunately, heart disease is largely preventable through early detection and proactive lifestyle changes.

Understanding the causes and symptoms of heart disease is crucial for prevention, early intervention, and improving overall health outcomes. When people are informed about common risk factors such as high blood pressure, poor diet, lack of physical activity, and smoking, they are more likely to adopt healthier habits and seek timely medical advice. Early signs like chest discomfort, shortness of breath, and unusual fatigue should never be ignored, as recognizing them early can lead to quicker diagnosis and effective treatment. Raising awareness and encouraging proactive care can significantly reduce the impact of heart disease in our communities.
